# 📊 Mindgruve Test (Business Case): Amber Road Channel Allocation Analysis

### 🎯 Project Objective

Amber Road, an enterprise software company, invests in three digital marketing channels: **Google Search**, **Facebook**, and **Display Advertising**. The marketing team received an additional budget and needs to determine **which channel should receive the incremental investment** to maximise business impact – measured through leads, qualified leads, and purchases.

This project analyses historical marketing spend and lead‑conversion data to provide a data‑driven recommendation.

---

### 📁 Repository Contents

| File | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **`data_science_test_data.xlsx`** | Raw data containing two sheets: <br> • **Sheet1** – daily marketing spend per channel + promotion flag <br> • **Sheet2** – lead‑level data (Lead Date, Qualified Date, Purchase Date) |
| **`mindgruve_main_analysis.ipynb`** | Main Jupyter Notebook. It contains:<br> • A detailed description of the entire project<br> • Data cleaning, merging, feature engineering, exploratory analysis, and linear regression modelling<br> • **Conclusion** answering the business question: *“Which channel should get the incremental budget and why?”*<br> • **Improvement suggestions** – additional data, information, or analyses that would make the recommendation stronger |
| **`mindgruve_supporting_answers.ipynb`** | Supporting notebook answering two specific questions:<br> • **A clear explanation of what was done** – how the data was used, how the models were built, and why the linear regression approach was chosen<br> • **Main results and interpretation** – what the numbers mean in business terms for Amber Road |
